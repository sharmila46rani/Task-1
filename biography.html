Career

After playing as a child artist in Vijetha (1985) and as a dancer in Daddy (2001), he made his adult lead debut in Gangotri.[28] The film was directed by K. Raghavendra Rao with his father Allu Aravind producing, along with C. Ashwini Dutt.[29] Praising his acting performance, Jeevi of Idlebrain criticised his looks in the film and added that "Arjun should choose roles that amplify his strength and nullify his weaknesses."[30] He then appeared in Sukumar's Arya.[31] He plays the eponymous lead, an outgoing and free-spirited boy falling in love with Geetha (Anu Mehta), an introverted girl who is on the shield of another person Ajay (Siva Balaji).[32] The film proved to be his breakthrough, earning a first nomination for the Filmfare Award for Best Actor – Telugu and won the Nandi Special Jury Award and the CineMAA Award for Best Actor (Critics).[33] The film was a critical and commercial success,[34] grossing over ₹30 crore,[35] with a production budget of ₹4 crore.[36] In 2006, the film was dubbed and released in Malayalam in Kerala. Owing to the film's success, he received major acclaim across the region and the Malayali people. He then obtained a degree in Bachelor of Business Administration at MSR College, Hyderabad On 6 March 2011, Allu Arjun married Sneha Reddy in Hyderabad.[173][174] They have two children—a son, Ayaan and a daughter, Arha.[173][175] Allu Arha made her debut in the film Shaakuntalam in the role of Prince Bharata.[176] In 2016, he started a nightclub named 800 Jubilee in collaboration with M Kitchens and Buffalo Wild Wings.[177] In 2019, Allu Arjun celebrated Sankranthi in his native town Palakollu, Andhra Pradesh and announced ₹20 lakh for the development and renovation works of the Ksheera Ramalingeswara Temple, one of the five Pancharama Kshetras.[178] The temple management has constructed Rathasala, Vahanasala, Gosala, and renovated the temple chariot with the funds provided.[179][180] He also campaigned for his uncle Pawan Kalyan's Jana Sena Party in Palakollu in the 2019 general elections.[181][182] On 30 November 2024, a FIR was filed against Allu Arjun by Srinivas Goud for his comments during the Mumbai press event, where Allu referred to his fans as an "army." Goud found this comparison disrespectful, arguing that using the term "army" in this context belittles the sacrifices made by the armed forces.[183][184] On 4 December 2024, a woman died and her young son was critically injured after a stampede occurred during the screening of Pushpa 2: The Rule at the Sandhya cinema hall in RTC X Roads, Hyderabad.[185] A case was later filed under various sections of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita on the film's unit, Allu Arjun, the owner of the cinema hall and Arjun's security team.[186][187] Allu was arrested by the Hyderabad City Police on 13 December 2024 over the said charges.[188] However, a few hours later he was granted an interim bail by the Telangana High Court.[189]

upcoming projects

He then starred in Puri Jagannadh's action film Desamuduru in which he played the role of Bala Govindam, a fearless journalist who falls for a woman with a darker past.[45] The film was a commercial success, fetching him a Santosham Film Award, a CineMAA Award and his second nomination for the Filmfare Award for Best Actor – Telugu.[46] His next film was Bhaskar's Parugu, where he played the role of Krishna, a happy-go-lucky man from Hyderabad who helps his friend to elope with his love, only to experience the wrath of the woman's father and the emotional struggle he felt. Idlebrain wrote: "Allu Arjun is pretty excellent in the first half as the characterization in the first half is vibrant and needs loads of energy. He carried the entire first half on his shoulders. He excelled in the emotional scenes in the second half."[47] Writing for Rediff.com, Radhikha Rajamani stated that "Arjun performs well though he is rather subdued."[48] Allu Arjun won his first Filmfare Award for Best Actor – Telugu and his second Nandi Special Jury Award. Allu Arjun next starred in Sukumar's action comedy Arya 2. A spiritual sequel to the romantic action film Arya (2004), he played the role of Arya, an orphan who is behaviourally sick in that he is consumed with possessiveness for his friend Ajay (Navdeep), who never accepts him. The film revolved around the complexities of love–hate relationships and love triangles.[49] His character in the film had shades of grey.[50] Sify wrote that "Allu Arjun is full of energy as the guy caught in the powerful current of love. Though he plays the part with negative shades, his characterization could evoke a lot of sympathy from the audiences. His dances are mind-blowing and he excels in emotional scenes."[51] Oneindia appreciated his dance moves and acting performances, especially the comedy scenes.[52] He received his fourth nomination for the Filmfare Award for Best Actor – Telugu for his performance in the film. He had two releases in 2010. The first was Gunasekhar's Varudu, in which he starred alongside Arya and Bhanu Sri Mehra.[53] The filmreceived mixed reviews from critics and was a box-office bomb.[54][55] His second release of the year was the highly acclaimed hyperlink anthology film Vedam. It was his first A-rated film in India, and the story takes an inspiration from 26/11 Mumbai blasts in Taj Mahal Palace Hotel, Mumbai.[56] He played the role of Anand "Cable" Raju, a cable operator hailing from Jubilee Hills (Hyderabad) slum. The film also features Anushka Shetty, Manchu Manoj and Manoj Bajpayee in other prominent roles.[57] His performance received appreciation from film critics,[58] with The Times of India calling it "scintillating"[59] and Idlebrain.com calling it as "best in his career".[60] A critic also commented that "He's a good dancer and does justice to his role."[61] Another critic wrote that "Allu Arjun is excellent as the groom who goes all out to get his bride back. His mass dialogues are good in the second half."[62] He achieved his second Filmfare Award for Best Actor – Telugu for the performance in the film.
